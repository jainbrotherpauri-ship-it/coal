Coal & Petroleum

The "Black Gold" of the earth. These fossil fuels power our industries and vehicles, but they are finite resources formed over millions of years.

Start Extraction

2. Coal

Coal is a hard, black, combustible mineral. It is the most widely used fossil fuel for generating electricity in thermal power plants.

Formation: Coal was formed millions of years ago when giant forests got buried under layers of soil. Due to high pressure and heat inside the earth, the buried plants slowly converted into coal. This process is called Carbonization.

3. History of Coal

Coal has been used as an energy source since ancient times. However, it became the primary fuel for the Industrial Revolution in the 18th and 19th centuries, powering steam engines and trains.

Useful Products of Coal

When coal is processed in the industry, several useful by-products are obtained.

4. Coke

Almost pure form of carbon. It is tougher than coal.

Uses: Used in the manufacture of steel and in the extraction of metals.

5. Coal Tar

A thick, black liquid with an unpleasant smell.

Uses: Used for making roads (bitumen), synthetic dyes, explosives, and perfumes (naphthalene balls).

6. Coal Gas

A mixture of hydrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide.

Uses: Used as a fuel in industries and for lighting in earlier times.

7. Petroleum

Petroleum is a dark, oily liquid with an unpleasant smell. It is also called "Black Gold".

Formation: It was formed from the remains of dead marine organisms (zooplankton and algae) buried under the ocean floor millions of years ago.

Extraction: Petroleum is extracted by drilling wells into the oil reservoirs.

8. Refining of Petroleum

Petroleum is a complex mixture of various hydrocarbons. It cannot be used as it is. It is refined in oil refineries by the process of Fractional Distillation to separate it into useful components.

Component / Fraction Boiling Range Uses
Petroleum Gas Below 40°C Fuel for home and industry (LPG).
Petrol 40°C to 70°C Motor fuel, dry cleaning solvent.
Kerosene 170°C to 250°C Fuel for stoves, lamps, and jet aircraft.
Diesel 250°C to 350°C Fuel for heavy motor vehicles, electric generators.
Lubricating Oil 350°C to 400°C Lubricants for machines, wax, vaseline.
Bitumen (Asphalt) Above 400°C Making roads, roofing.

9. Natural Gas

Natural Gas is a very important fossil fuel found along with petroleum. It is easy to transport through pipes.

Composition: It is a mixture of Methane (85%), ethane, and propane.

Uses:

  • Used as a fuel in homes and industries (CNG).
  • Used as a raw material in the petrochemical industry.
  • Used for generating electricity.

Clean Fuel

Natural Gas is considered a cleaner fuel than coal or petroleum because it burns easily and produces less smoke and harmful gases.

10. Some Natural Resources are Limited

Coal, petroleum, and natural gas are Fossil Fuels. They take millions of years to form. Human beings are consuming them at a very fast rate.

At this rate of consumption, these fuels will be exhausted in the near future. Therefore, we must use them judiciously and conserve them.

11. Types of Resources

Resources can be broadly classified based on their availability:

12. Renewable Resources

Resources that can be replenished or reproduced by physical, chemical, or mechanical processes in a short period of time.

☀️ Solar 💨 Wind 💧 Water

Examples: Air, Water, Sunlight, Forests.

13. Non-Renewable Resources

Resources that are present in limited quantities. Once consumed, they cannot be replenished by natural processes in a short period.

🪨 Coal 🛢️ Petroleum ⛽ Natural Gas

Examples: Minerals, Fossil Fuels, Metals.
